Couteau Santoku

Extremely versatile, the Santoku knife is a must-have among Japanese knives. The name Santoku comes from Japanese and means 3 virtues, suggesting its ability to cut vegetables, meat and fish. A distant cousin of the Western chef's knife, the Santoku knife is generally shorter and lighter, making it a more manageable cutting tool and very popular with professionals and amateurs alike. Often compared with its Japanese counterpart, the Gyuto knife, the Santoku is shorter, lighter and above all less expensive, which makes it an ideal first knife for a cook.

Couteaux Yanagiba

The Yanagiba is a long Japanese kitchen knife traditionally used to slice sashimi. It is sometimes called the katana of the kitchen in reference to the famous Japanese sword. A Yanagiba has a long blade with which you can slice a piece of meat or fish in one motion. Without having to make sawing movements, for the best result. A real must have for sushi lovers. Perfect for slicing beautiful maki rolls in one go or for sashimi on nigiri. In addition to sashimi, a Yanagiba is also perfect for slicing thinly smoked ham or carpaccio.

Couteaux Nakiri

The nakiri knife is a traditional Japanese vegetable knife. Vegetable knives in Japan are known as Nakiri (bilateral sharpening) or Usuba (unilateral sharpening). A nakiri is recognizable by its rectangular shape. The tip of the knife is slightly rounded in order to cut the vegetables quickly. This knife can be used to cut carrots, cucumbers, cabbage and other vegetables finely and effortlessly. Chopping herbs is also a breeze. Nakiri knives are really popular and it's no wonder. In an age where healthy living is very important, we cut many more vegetables than usual.
